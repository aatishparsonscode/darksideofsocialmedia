Deepfakes

Deep Fakes are a tool to make videos that resemble legitimate events but are actually edited - similar to photoshop. The primary use-case so far has been in pornography, but they can also be used to make videos of anyone - including influential figures like politicians and celebrities - where they can be portrayed saying damaging things. The visual and audio components of these videos can be faked using a form of artificial intelligence called deep learning.

Overview
Although there are potential cost saving benefits, deep fakes pose a serious threat to the general public, something the general public should be made aware of.

Why this topic?
This is important to us because deepfakes are a form of misinformation many of us face day-to-day which go completely undetected. Unlike how we have been trained to spot shady information given to us in a text format, seeing visual and audio fakes are brand new and an especially large threat given how we have a “see it to believe it” mentality.
